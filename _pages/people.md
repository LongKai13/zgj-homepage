## Group

Our group works at the intersection of urban science, transportation, and artificial intelligence. We develop AI methods for understanding, representing, and interacting with complex urban spatio-temporal systems. In particular, we are interested in building models that can integrate multimodal urban data, learn generalizable urban representations, and enable reliable embodied intelligence in real-world city environments. Our current research directions include multimodal reasoning for cities, urban foundation representation models, and trustworthy embodied urban AI.

<h2 id="openings">Openings</h2>

I am actively recruiting highly motivated undergraduate students, research interns, and prospective Master’s (2027) and Ph.D. (2027) students to work on research problems in urban AI, transportation, and spatio-temporal data science. Our group develops AI methods for understanding, representing, and interacting with complex urban spatio-temporal environments.

Current research topics in the group include multimodal reasoning with maps, trajectories, imagery, and text; scalable and transferable urban foundation representation models; and reliable, safe, and transparent embodied AI systems for real-world urban environments. Prospective students and collaborators with related interests are welcome to get in touch.

### What I value in prospective students

- Strong interest in research and curiosity about urban, transportation, and spatio-temporal problems
- Self-motivation, reliability, and the ability to work through open-ended research questions
- Solid analytical, mathematical, or technical skills, depending on the applicant’s background
- Students from diverse academic backgrounds are encouraged to apply

### Why join the group

- Close mentoring on research ideas, problem formulation, experimental design, and academic writing
- Opportunities to work on meaningful problems in urban computing, transportation systems, and intelligent decision-making
- A research environment that values both methodological depth and real-world relevance

### How to reach out

Please email [gjzheng@sjtu.edu.cn](mailto:gjzheng@sjtu.edu.cn) with your CV and a short note describing your background, interests, and why you would like to join the group.

If relevant, you may also include coursework, projects, publications, or code repositories that best represent your work.
